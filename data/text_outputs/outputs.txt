CPI and SLN Sustainability collaborated with the Design for Sustainability and Circularity (DfSC) framework to redesign a toddler's water bottle.
The project aimed to balance safety, functionality, and sustainability from the start.
Using a data-driven approach, the team selected materials based on drinking water safety, impact resistance, manufacturability, and environmental performance.
A Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) was integrated early to evaluate environmental impacts against planetary boundaries.
The final concept minimized material variety, using only two components, reducing production waste and simplifying end-of-life processes.
